We're Fighting for Change in Family Courts Because the System Hurts More Than Just Us

Our story, while painful, isn't unique. We've spent over a decade navigating a family court system that's made the process more drawn-out, painful, and costly than it needed to be. My husband and I, like many other families, entered the system in hopes of a resolution that was fair and in the best interest of our children.

What we found was a system that thrives on inefficiency, one that allows manipulation and delay to reign over facts, logic, and the best interests of those who matter most—our children. The longer families stay in the system, the more profit there is to be made. From countless court appearances to endless legal consultations, the process seems built to perpetuate conflict rather than resolve it.

But here's the thing: while our experience has been difficult, we know it pales in comparison to the nightmare others face every day. 41% of first marriages end in divorce, and that figure jumps to 60% for second marriages and 73% for third marriages. More than 36% of weddings involve one spouse who has been married before, yet our family courts are still stuck operating in the 1950s. It's time we face the facts—our family court system is designed for a world that no longer exists.

We're not here to air grievances or point fingers. We're sharing our story because it's just one example of how this system fails families every day. We want people to understand that this issue isn't personal; it's systemic. We're advocating for change not because of what's happened to us, but because of what happens to thousands of families like ours across the country. Families who are left in limbo, fighting emotional battles that the system profits from, rather than healing.

The Solution is Already Here: AI Can Fix What's Broken

Imagine a world where family courts operated efficiently. Where cases were resolved quickly, fairly, and based on facts, rather than emotional manipulation or who can afford to drag out the process. This isn't a fantasy—it's possible with AI.

AI can help analyze legal communications for bias or emotional exploitation, allowing families to focus on what really matters: the well-being of their children. AI could streamline decision-making processes, reduce delays, and ensure that outcomes are based on facts rather than drawn-out emotional disputes.

This isn't about removing humans from the process—it's about adding fairness, transparency, and efficiency. It's about putting families first, not profit.

Why This Message Matters: It's About More Than Us

We're not alone in this fight. Millions of families have been affected by a system that's outdated and inefficient. And that's exactly why this issue is so important to us. It's not about what my husband's ex-wife did or didn't do—it's about the fact that this system allows emotional and legal manipulation to thrive.

We're here to fight for reform, for a future where families can resolve their issues without spending years in courtrooms, without having their children caught in the middle, and without being exploited by a broken system.

We're not asking for special treatment, and we're certainly not trying to benefit personally from this advocacy. We're simply using our experience as a lens through which to view a larger, more serious problem. We know that for every family like ours, there are countless others whose stories are even more painful.

Why AI is the Common-Sense Solution

At its core, AI can bring objectivity and clarity to a system that often lacks both. AI can help identify when emotional exploitation is being used as a tactic to delay or manipulate court proceedings, allowing judges to focus on the facts. It's not about replacing humans—it's about assisting them in making decisions that are fair, transparent, and timely.

We understand the fears around AI. But the truth is, if we don't embrace this technology, the family court system will continue to fail families. AI can eliminate delays, reduce costs, and most importantly, remove emotional manipulation from legal proceedings—things that are long overdue.

The Bigger Picture: It's Time for Reform

We're speaking out because we want change—not just for us, but for every family that has been caught in this system. We're ready to embrace technology, to modernize the system, and to make family courts work the way they were always supposed to—for families.

We believe in a future where families can navigate life's challenges without being exploited by a system that profits from delay and emotional suffering. We're advocating for reform not just because of what we've been through, but because we know that families everywhere deserve better.
